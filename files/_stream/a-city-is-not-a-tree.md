Semi-lattice vs tree. This essay keeps resurfacing in everything I read about complexity.

Alexander's point is that real cities are semi-lattices — overlapping, messy, richly connected — while planners keep drawing trees: clean hierarchies that kill the life they are trying to organize. The same tension shows up in software architecture, in organizations, in product roadmaps.

I am trying to notice when I am forcing a tree structure on something that wants to be a semi-lattice. Usually the tree feels cleaner in the short term and dies in the long term.
