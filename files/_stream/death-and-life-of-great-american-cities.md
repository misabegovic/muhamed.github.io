Jacobs' argument keeps haunting me: cities are not problems to be solved by planners. They are ecosystems of human interaction.

What struck me most is how much of her critique applies to software communities too. We often try to engineer culture from the top — add a process, a tool, a manifesto — and then wonder why the life drains out. Healthy communities, like healthy neighborhoods, grow from small interactions, trust built over time, and a mix of different people doing different things at different hours.

I keep coming back to this whenever I walk through Vienna and notice which streets feel alive and which feel like stage sets.
