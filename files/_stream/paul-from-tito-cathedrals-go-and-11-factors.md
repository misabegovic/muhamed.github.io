Just spoke with Paul from [Tito](https://ti.to/home). He's building cathedrals now — long-term, careful software — and he's not building in Ruby anymore. His current stack is Go, and he has a fresh, principled take on how systems should be built.

He pointed me to [11factor.org](https://11factor.org/), his update to the twelve-factor app for the AI era. A few that hit hard:

- **V. Intent is the system** — the value is the ideas, and reality measured against them. Source code is regenerable; intent is not.
- **VII. Self-hosting is a right** — if you can't run it yourself, you don't own it.
- **VIII. Many small things** — greater than one big thing. Comprehensible, auditable, resilient.
- **X. Humans come first** — AI is opt-in, never required, always disclosed.

His current project is [Eleven Messenger](https://home.elevenmessenger.com/), and the unfiltered thinking is at [elevenmessenger.com/unfiltered](https://elevenmessenger.com/unfiltered/).

We align on a lot. I have a lot to learn here.
