Separating decision quality from outcome quality is harder than it looks. We are all bad at it.

Duke's framework — thinking in bets — is useful not because it makes you a better gambler, but because it gives you language for uncertainty. In tech we love to pretend we are playing chess: if we just plan well enough, the right move will produce the right outcome. Most important decisions are poker. You can do everything right and still lose because of hidden information and luck.

The practice I am trying to build: review decisions, not just outcomes. Ask "what would I do again?" instead of "what worked?"
