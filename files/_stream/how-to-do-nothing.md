The attention economy is not just about distraction. It is about whose time is being sold and for what.

Odell's argument is not about meditation or productivity hacks. It is about refusing to let every moment of your life be harvested for someone else's metric. That refusal is political, ecological, and deeply personal.

I think about this a lot when I catch myself reaching for my phone in a gap between tasks. The gap is not broken. The gap is where thinking happens.
